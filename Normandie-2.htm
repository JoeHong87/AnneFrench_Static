..Paris to Normandie....



The glass pyramid in the center court yard of Louvre - designed by I.M. Pei - Chinese American architect -
it created so much controversy - now everybody loves it. The concept crossed the boundaries of ancient and modern,
East and West, classic and contemporary. It is a project of the century.
 

Spending half an hour taking a walk in the court yard of Louvre
gives me a very good feeling of composure to start the day.
I like the crisp sound when I walk on the unpaved sandy ground.
 		  


Looking at this inverted pyramid, I was trying to find some clues of beginning of  modern art - that becomes more and more difficult to comprehend. I am just pretending that I found the clue. Non the less I appreciate modern art sometimes.
 



I am on top of Grand Boulevard in my temporary office.
 

Coffee is 1.30 Euro and I have free internet connection at McDonald.
This is what I call American convenience in the heart of Paris.


        
Grand Boulevard by Jean Pierre Dubord

It takes two and half hours by train to get to Normandie from Paris.
I loved the diffused light and tranquility. I drove through the winding country road.
 		  

Jean Pierre Dubord, the native of Normandie,
he is a descendant of Ecole de Rouen. He carries the torch
of French impressionism to the next generation. 
 

 


View from Jean Pierre Dubord's atelier		  

View from Jean Pierre Dubord's atelier



Jean Pierre and his wife Evelyne serving welcoming Champaign for me.
The salon was decorated with Japanese paintings. I found out he has two shows in Japan every year. He is very popular in Japan.
While Jean Pierre is painting Evelyne is working for women's charity or studying symbolism. I almost asked her whether she knows professor Langdon.


 		  

 
A major exhibition called "Impressionists from Rouen" - is being organized for this summer. Paintings from impressionist masters who painted in Rouen including
Monet, Pissarro, Sisley and many others will be exhibited and the works of Jean Pierre Dubord who represents the living artist - will also be exhibited.
He is very busy these days for preparing this project.
