Fernand Herbo (1905-1995)

  Fernand Herbo is a second generation of French Impressionist masters from Normandie area. He is a son of railroadman, studied art in Paris and moved to Honfleur area in 1930. He liked to
  depict the nature when it is not calm - always there is a movement of windy sky and choppy water. It could be his inner state of emotion. Brush stroke is spontaneous and reminds me
  Vincent Van Gogh. He became Official French Marine Artist (honorary title given by French Navy to the artist who paints the sea) in 1944. He was discovered and represented by Katia Granoff -
  the famous French art dealer and Secretary of Salon d'Autome. His paintings are in the permanent collection of some of the French museums - including Marine Museum.

Jacques Bouyssou  (1926 - 1997)
 Moret sur Loing

Bouyssou captured the crisp atmosphere of  beginning pf autumn  in Moret sur Loing - outside Paris. The beauty of this region inspired many artists - specially  Monet, Renoir and Sisley.  You can view Alfred Sisley's painting of Moret sur Loing on the Web Museum. In this painting - the brush stroke of Bouyssou is spontaneous, free and sincere.  He just instilled the beauty of the surrounding area in this canvas. The composition is wonderful with Seine river with bridge and train just crossed. The house with red roof and mother and child are strolling on the river bank.
