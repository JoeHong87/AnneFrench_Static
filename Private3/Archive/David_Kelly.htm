I put three Bouyssou paintings on your living room wall.
The paintings are quite different from each other. The first one depict Honfleur basin and it is very tranquil and the weather is balmy. Peaceful painting well balanced. If you ever go to Honfleur you will love this painting more.

Jacques Bouyssou
(1926 - 1997)
 Tempete a Ostende

The wave is choppy and wind is blowing. This is the harbor entrance of Ostende Belgium next to Holland. Bouyssou as well as many other French impressionist painters frequented this area to paint the beach. The French collectors like the stormy skies and waves on the ocean while American collectors like sun drenched beaches  on balmy weather. Fishing boats sailing out and a lone figure is running alongside the waters' edge/ Seagulls flying around the light house. I can almost breeze the ocean smell.




Oil on canvas -  29"  x  36 1/4" - painted 1974      -   frame  - 38" x 45 1/4"
