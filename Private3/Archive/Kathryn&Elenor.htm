Jacques Bouyssou  (1926 - 1997)
 Moret sur Loing

Bouyssou captured the crisp atmosphere of  beginning pf autumn  in Moret sur Loing - outside Paris. The beauty of this region inspired many artists - specially  Monet, Renoir and Sisley.  You can view Alfred Sisley's painting of Moret sur Loing on the Web Museum. In this painting - the brush stroke of Bouyssou is spontaneous, free and sincere.  He just instilled the beauty of the surrounding area in this canvas. The composition is wonderful with Seine river with bridge and train just crossed. The house with red roof and mother and child are strolling on the river bank. Jacques Bouyssou
(1926 - 1997)

Tempete a Ostende
 Listed below is a beautiful oil painting by Jacques Bouyssou.  This painting depicts Belgian fishing port Ostende with fishing boats on choppy water and the sea gulls flying in the high wind. There are people on the jetty watching the stormy sea. Jacques Bouyssou frequented port of Ostende and produced beautiful paintings. He loved to paint seascapes visiting different areas and ports of France and other parts Europe, and eventually he earned the title of Official Marine Painter of France and participated annual exhibition of marine paintings at the marine museum in Paris - Musee de la Marine.
