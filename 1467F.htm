Kyu Nam Han

Times Square in Blue




 Acrylic on canvas     43 3/4" x 56 1/4"   (111 x 143 cm) painted 2007 - 2008

Michel Henry

Paris en Bleu



Oil on canvas - 35" x 45 3/4" painted 1991
won gold medal - salon violet

I am thrilled to be able to showcase these two paintings - Times Square in Blue & Paris en Bleu - side by side. Kyu Nam Han and Michel Henry never met. Kyu Nam Han is a descendant of the New York School and Michel Henry is from the Ecole de Paris. Kyu Nam Han just finished this painting "Times Square in Blue". He is in his mid-sixties. Michel Henry was in his mid sixties when he painted Paris en Bleu. Both artist are in the peak of their maturity. They are mellow and romantic - and a bit nostalgic. One can see the virtuosity of the masters'  brush stroke of throwing colors on canvas. The color blue typically translates to tranquil and sadness. Kyu Nam Han put the square image of the LG sign in red to break this sentiment of triste and Michel Henry put a pink  bouquet of flowers to break away from the emotion of triste.  In Paris en Bleu - the Seine river flows by the eternal Notre Dame and in Time Square - the yellow cabs are moving in heavy traffic in front of the building where the ball drops at mid-night on New Years' Eve. In Paris en Bleu - the sky is tinted with soft yellow, pink and grey with a late afternoon sun and these tonalities put the images and sentiments in harmony. In Times Square in Blue, the yellow tint around the clock building and tint of pink purple in the sky settle down the noise level on the street and put the pictorial images in harmony. I just love these paintings.  -john hong -

Listed here is a beautiful  original painting on canvas by Kyu Nam Han depicting Time Square - New York City.   Kyu Nam Han is a New York artist who developed a unique style and  his works are collected by serious art collectors. For more information about his art - please click - Kyu Nam Han.       You can also read the article on him by   New York Times   

"Han notes that he likes to think of his compositions as visual analogies to a string quartet in which individual elements - line, hue, color, or texture - are allowed to dominate or recede in the interests of the larger effect. Images hover above or below the surface, present and absent at the same time. Through such devices, Han manages to express the sense of New York City as an amalgamation of fleeting sights, sounds and ever changing energy." -Eleanor Heartney                                     

The diversity effects which Han achieves in this painting is quite striking. He gives stimulating visual effect  by breaking down the forms in mosaics of colors and hold them in grids. He deconstructs the imageries and reconstructs them to give his artistic expressions.
