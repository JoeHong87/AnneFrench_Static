Claude Pissarro

St. James Park

 

Oil on Canvas  -  23 5/8" x 28 3/4"

 

 

 

 

This painting was exhibited in Seoul Korea in special exhibition sponsored by Korea Daily News in 1995.
$14,500.


 

This is  absolutely beautiful landscape  by Claude Pissarro, following the tradition set by his famous grandfather Camille Pissarro who started the impressionism.  The dappling brush stroke is the reminiscence of Camille Pissarro’s famous master pieces.  Here Claude Pissarro depicts St. James Park in London.  You are looking at the old  buildings across the lake where people leisurely enjoys boating.  When I was visiting London, I went to St. James Park to find the vantage point of this scene where the artist was painting. It was on the small bridge crossing the lake. I looked back and I saw the Buckingham Palace. The scenery with the people leisurely enjoys boating on weekend afternoon is truly beautiful and peaceful.

When you look at above close-up image, you will see the brush stroke of the artist that looks like crochet interlocking with millions of colors. This is truly wonderful stroke that came down generations in the Pissarro family. It is such a joy to watch myriads of colors interlocking each other and dancing.                  

Claude Pissarro was born in 1935.  He is a grandson of Camille Pissarro and son of Paulemile Pissarro. Camille taught Paulemille to paint and Monet was a godfather of Paulemille  and influenced with his artistic vision.   Claude followed his father on his painting excursions.  Now he carries on the legacy left by Camille Pissarro, one of the initiator of  Impressionism.
