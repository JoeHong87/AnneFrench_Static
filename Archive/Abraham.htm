Jacques Bouyssou
(1926 - 1997)

...Paris - Rue Durantin...


You are looking at a beautiful oil painting of Jacques Bouyssou depicting a street of Paris – Rue Durantin – with people strolling on the street. Many artist painted Paris streets and buildings. The building façade is well rendered with personalities. Utrillo painted many back streets of Paris and his painting shows a feeling of detachment  and loneliness while Bouyssou is very unpretentious and happy. Bouyssou was a very simple and happy person – and he conveys his inner feeling to the viewer. He depicts everyday life on the streets of Paris. It is like listening a chanson or reading a small poetry sitting at the bistro sipping aperitif.

Jacques Bouyssou
(1926 - 1997)

Amsterdam - Les Cafes


 Listed below is a beautiful oil painting by Jacques Bouyssou.  This painting depicts  a street of Paris with old buildings and people casually strolling.  The spectrum of mellow colors on the store fronts of the buildings gives pictorial charm.       The characters of the facade of the old buildings give feeling of mellowness like aging wine.  Through this painting with masterly touch of Bouyssou  the viewers sense the simple and yet happy Parisian life.  This is painting for true collector.  I remember a remark by a collector who said " Jacques Bouyssou is happy Utrillo."

Jacques Bouyssou
(1926 - 1997)

Automne en Normandie




Oil on Canvas  -  25 5/8" x 31 7/8"

 Bouyssou loved nature like other impressionist masters. This is very serene scenery. Two people walking down the country lane on the hill looking down the sea in Normandy. It is in autumn with cool air. Seagulls are flying and there are fishing boats far away.  I have this painting behind the lamp table - when I look at it at the end of the day - the tranquility sets in me.
